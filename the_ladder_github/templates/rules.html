Rules

These are the working rules for THE LADDER proof-of-concept.

Weekly challenge format

Each week, shooters challenge the person directly above or below them depending on the weekly attacking setting.

  • If even positions attack, position 2 attacks 1, position 4 attacks 3, position 6 attacks 5, and so on.
  • If odd positions attack, position 3 attacks 4, position 5 attacks 6, position 7 attacks 8, and so on.
  • Position 1 never attacks. Position 1 only defends when position 2 is attacking.
  • The lowest placed shooter only attacks when their position is part of the attacking group. If there is nobody below them to defend, they have no match that week.

Winning and movement

  • The attacking shooter must outscore the defending shooter.
  • If the attacker scores higher, the attacker and defender swap ladder positions.
  • If the attacker ties or scores lower, the ladder positions stay the same.
  • If either shooter does not have a score entered for that week, that match is not processed.

Scores

  • Scores are entered out of 100.10 for 25 yard indoor.
  • Valid examples include 98.07, 90.09, 100.07, and 100.10.
  • Only 100 can have .10.
  • A score with .09 must be at least 90.09, .08 must be at least 80.08, .07 must be at least 70.07, and so on.
  • Each shooter can only have one score entered per week.

Score entry window

  • Scores are entered for the current week.
  • The week runs from Monday to Sunday.
  • The ladder auto-processes completed weeks after the Sunday cutoff when the app is running, or when the app is next opened after the cutoff.

Club access

  • Members must log in using their club account before viewing the ladder.
  • Club accounts can only enter scores for shooters registered to their own club.
  • TSNZ numbers are shown only inside the admin and score-entry area.

Super admin controls

  • The super admin can create club accounts.
  • The super admin can register, remove, and manage shooters.
  • The super admin can delete scores.
  • The super admin can change this week’s attacking setting between odd positions attacking and even positions attacking.

Grades and age status

  • Grades are D, C, B, A, and Master.
  • When a shooter starts in a new grade, they start at the bottom of that grade.
  • Junior is calculated by year: if a shooter turns 21 before December of that year, they are not a junior for the following year.
  • Veteran is 60 and above.
  • All other shooters are standard.
